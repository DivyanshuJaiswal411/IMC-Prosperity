The challenge required building a Python-based trading engine to interact with a live, adversarial matching engine, managing position limits, and optimizing execution strategies.

## 🧠 Core Trading Strategies

The algorithm trades two highly distinct assets, requiring two completely different mathematical approaches:

### 1. Ash-Coated Osmium (Market Making & Inventory Management)
Osmium exhibited highly stable, mean-reverting behavior. The primary risk was inventory accumulation (hitting the absolute limit of `80` units and getting sidelined).
* **Strategy:** An Avellaneda-Stoikov-inspired dynamic market-making model.
* **Execution:** The bot calculates a "reservation price" based on the market mid-price, skewed by current inventory levels. If the bot is holding a heavily long position, it aggressively lowers both its Bid and Ask quotes to offload inventory while continuing to capture the spread.

### 2. Intarian Pepper Root (Statistical Arbitrage & Spread Sniping)
Pepper Root was highly volatile. Initial attempts at aggressive execution resulted in heavy losses due to the "spread tax" (paying the market maker 15-20 XIRECS per trade).
* **Strategy:** Z-Score Mean Reversion. The bot tracks a 40-period Volume Weighted Exponential Moving Average (EMA) and calculates a rolling standard deviation. 
* **Execution (The Squeeze):** Trades are only triggered when the Z-score exceeds `1.25` (the mathematical "Golden Mean" between volume and accuracy). To avoid the spread tax, the bot executes as a **Spread Sniper**—quoting exactly `best_bid + 1` and `best_ask - 1`. It refuses to cross the spread, ensuring high capital efficiency.

### 3. Game Theory: Market Access Fee (MAF)
Round 2 introduced a blind auction where the top 50% of bidders paid a one-time fee to access 25% extra order flow.
* **Logic:** Based on offline local simulations, the base algorithm generated substantial profit. The MAF was calculated as a conservative ~10% of the expected theoretical value of the extra flow, resulting in a safe bid of `50 XIRECS` that secured extra volume without eroding net margins.

## 📊 Performance & Logs

Transitioning from an offline backtester (which assumes infinite liquidity) to the live matching engine required critical recalibration. By shifting from aggressive market-taking to passive spread-sniping, the algorithm successfully achieved a stable, positive expectancy.

* **Final Live PnL:** `+1,386 XIRECS`
* **Execution Style:** Passive/Maker (High Win-Rate, Low Spread-Tax)
